Directions

In this simulation, heat flux is measured through a composite wall, with a length of 10 cm and a fixed temperature of 45°C at the right wall. You can adjust the left wall temperature, the thickness of the glass, concrete, and stainless steel walls using the sliders. The right wall material can be chosen with the dropdown menu. Press the Details button to examine the governing equations used in this simulation.

Details
The equation for heat flux is:
$$ q’’ = \frac{T_0 \ – \ T_4}{R_{total}} $$
Where \(T_0 \) is the temperature at the left wall, \(T_4 \) is the temperature at the right wall (45°C), and \(R_{total} \) is the combined resistance of the four wall segments (\(cm^2 K/W\)). The equation for \(R_{total} \) is:
$$ R_{total} = \sum_{i=1}^4 \frac{x_i}{k_i} $$
Where \(x_i \) is the thickness of each wall segment (cm) and \(k_i \) is the thermal conductivity of the wall’s material (W/[cm K]). The thermal conductivities of the materials are:
$$ k_{glass} = 0.0096 \ W/[cm \ K] $$
$$ k_{concrete} = 0.014 \ W/[cm \ K] $$
$$ k_{steel} = 0.163 \ W/[cm \ K] $$
$$ k_{fiberglass} = 0.0004 \ W/[cm \ K] $$
$$ k_{brick} = 0.014 \ W/[cm \ K] $$
$$ k_{lead} = 0.35 \ W/[cm \ K] $$
