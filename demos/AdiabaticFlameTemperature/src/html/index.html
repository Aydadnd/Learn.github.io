Fuel and air are fed to an adiabatic furnace. One of five hydrocarbons can be selected as the fuel. The fuel undergoes complete combustion in the furnace to form CO2 and H2O. The energy released by the reaction raises the temperature of the exhaust gases. An energy balance is used to calculate the temperature of the exhaust gases (the adiabatic flame temperature)1. The feed temperatures of fuel and O2 (or air) can be selected with sliders, and the amount of excess O2 (or excess air) can also be selected with sliders. Tflame estimates may be higher than reported in literature due to this simulation's omission of endothermic product dissociation at high temperatures2. Based on a simulation by Binous et al.3
