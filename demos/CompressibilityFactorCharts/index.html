Directions

The compressibility factor chart plots the compressibility factor \(Z\), equal to \(\frac{PV}{RT}\), where \(V\) is the volume per mole, versus the reduced pressure \(P_r\) for several values of the reduced temperature \(T_r\). The reduced pressure and temperature are defined by \(P_t = P/P_c\) and \(T_r = T/T_c\), respectively where \(P_c\) is the critical pressure and \(T_c\) is the critical temperature. Use buttons to select one of five molecules, and move the black dot to display the compressibility factor curve (blue) for any value \(T_r\) between 1.0 and 1.8. The \(Z\) and \(P_r\) values for the location of the black dot are displayed on the chart. For an ideal gas, \(Z\) = 1 (green line).

Details
The Soave modification of the Redlich-Kwong (SRK) equation of state is used to calculate the compressibility factor:
$$ P = \frac{RT}{V-b} - \frac{a \alpha}{V(V+b)}. $$
This equation can be written in terms of the reduced temperature \(T_r = T/T_c\):
$$ P = \frac{RT_rT_c}{V-b} - \frac{a \alpha}{V(V-b)}, $$
where \(P\) is pressure (bar), \(R\) is the ideal gas constant (cm3bar/mol K), \(T\) is temperature (K), \(T_c\) is the critical temperature (K), \(V\) is volume (cm3/mol), and \(a\), \(b\), and \(\alpha\) are constants:
$$ a = 0.427\frac{R^2T_c^2}{P_c}\alpha, $$
$$ b = 0.087\frac{RT_c}{P_c}, $$
$$ \alpha = (1 + \kappa(1-\sqrt{T_r}))^2, $$
where \(P_c\) is the critical pressure \(\kappa = 0.48 + 1.574\omega - 0.176\omega^2\), and \(\omega\) is the acentric factor.
The compressibility factor is defined by:
$$ Z = \frac{PV}{RT}. $$
