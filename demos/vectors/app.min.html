Vector addition is used to sum together multiple vectors of the same dimension. The resultant vector is defined by the sum of the individual components of each vector. For example, if we have two vectors: $$\vec{u}=2\hat{\imath}+3\hat{\jmath}$$ $$\vec{v}=4\hat{\imath}+7\hat{\jmath}$$ then their sum is: $$\vec{u}+\vec{v}=(2+4)\hat{\imath}+(3+7)\hat{\jmath}=6\hat{\imath}+10\hat{\jmath}$$ Other forms of vector notation to express this sum include matrix notation: $$\vec{u}+\vec{v}=\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}+\begin{bmatrix}4 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}6 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}$$ $$\vec{u}+\vec{v}=\begin{bmatrix}2 & 3 \end{bmatrix}+\begin{bmatrix}4 & 7 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}6 & 10 \end{bmatrix}$$ Ordered-set notation: $$\langle x \rangle$$
