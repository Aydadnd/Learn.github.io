This simulation shows the velocity profile of fluid in pipe.

Details

A fully developed laminar flow in horizontal pipe has the following properties: (1) The fluid is only moving in z direction. (2) The system does not change over time. (3) Fluid have zero velocity on pipe walls. (4) Velocity is constant down the streamline. (5) There is no axial mixing. (6) The fluid velocity is at maximum at the center of the pipe. In our simulation, we can also assume that (7)the effect from gravitational force is neglectable.

The Navier-Stokes equation for cylindrical coordinate in z direction is: $$ \begin{multline} \rho (\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial t} + u_r \frac{\partial u_r}{\partial r} + \frac{u_\theta}{r}\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial_\theta} - \frac{u^2_\theta}{r} + u_z \frac{\partial u_r}{\partial z}) \\ = - \frac{\partial p}{\partial z} + \rho g_z + \mu ( \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial}{\partial r}(r\frac{\partial u_z}{\partial r}) + \frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial ^2 u_z}{\partial \theta ^2} + \frac{\partial ^2 u_z}{\partial z^2} ) \end{multline} $$ Based on properties above, the equation can be simplified to: $$ 0 = - \frac{\partial p}{\partial z} + \mu \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}(r \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial r}) $$ In this simulation, \(\frac{\partial p}{\partial z}\) equals to \(\frac{\Delta p}{l}\), which is a constant value. The equation can be solved with the following boundary conditions: $$ u_z(R) = 0 $$ $$ \left. \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial r} \right|_{r=0} = 0 $$ Where \(R\) is the distance from the center of the pipe to the pipe wall. The final solution for \(u_z\) is: $$ u_z = \frac{R^2}{4 \mu} \frac{\Delta p}{l} (1-\frac{r^2}{R^2}) $$
